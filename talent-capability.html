Point of View

Talent acquisition is not enough.

The market often frames technology talent as a sourcing problem. CoreCloud frames it as a capability creation problem.

Organisations need architects, engineers, developers, analysts, operators and leaders who can translate strategy into execution. The gap is rarely only headcount. It is the alignment between skills, leadership, operating model and delivery context.

This is why CoreCloud connects Talent & Capability to the broader platform. Technology, venue decisions, governance and transformation all require people who can execute.

Executive Questions

Questions that reveal capability gaps.

Capability planning should be tied to business outcomes, not generic job titles.

What capability is missing?Define the work that must be performed before defining the role to hire.
Which capability should be internal?Determine what must be owned, what can be sourced and what can be delivered through partners.
Where is leadership required?Identify whether execution gaps are skill gaps, coordination gaps or decision gaps.
Can the operating model absorb talent?Great people fail inside weak structures. Capability requires context.
How will capability scale?Use talent ecosystems and marketplaces to expand capacity without losing control.
