The Shift

The economics problem

Modern technology costs are variable, distributed and increasingly difficult to govern.

Public cloud, data services, AI workloads, SaaS applications and consumption-based infrastructure have changed how technology spend behaves.

As environments grow, visibility decreases. As visibility decreases, waste increases. As waste increases, confidence in technology investment declines.

Definition

What is FinOps?

FinOps is the discipline of aligning technology consumption with business value.

Financial Accountability

Understand where money is being spent and why.

Operational Visibility

Understand how platforms are consumed.

Technical Optimisation

Ensure workloads operate efficiently.

Governance

Establish controls that support sustainable growth.

Business Alignment

Connect technology investment to measurable outcomes.

Framework

The CoreCloud FinOps Framework

CoreCloud evaluates technology economics across five dimensions.

VisibilityWhat are we spending, where are we spending, and which workloads generate cost?
AccountabilityWho owns each workload, budget and consumption decision?

Venue Economics

FinOps and workload venue

The right venue often creates the largest optimisation opportunity.

Public Cloud

Excellent for elastic demand and rapid innovation, but requires strong consumption governance.

Private Cloud

Effective for predictable utilisation and controlled economics.

Sovereign Infrastructure

Supports regulated workloads where governance requirements influence economics.

Hybrid Models

Enable mixed workload portfolios but require disciplined financial controls.
