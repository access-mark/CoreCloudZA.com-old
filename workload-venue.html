The challenge

Technology decisions are no longer binary.

The question is not whether an organisation should adopt public cloud, private cloud, sovereign infrastructure, hosted platforms or AI environments. The question is which venue delivers the best business outcome.

For more than a decade, technology strategy has been influenced by broad mandates: cloud first, SaaS first, AI first and digital first.

Those mandates accelerated adoption, but they also created cost volatility, governance complexity, data sovereignty exposure and duplicated capability.

CoreCloud evaluates every workload against business outcomes rather than platform ideology.

Framework

The six-dimension venue framework

Every workload is assessed through a consistent decision model.

01

Economics

Consumption profile, cost predictability, long-term TCO, licensing impact and data movement costs.

02

Performance

Latency, throughput, geographic proximity, resilience and user experience requirements.

03

Sovereignty

Data residency, POPIA alignment, jurisdiction, regulatory obligations and control requirements.

04

Security

Threat model, data sensitivity, access controls, operational security and risk exposure.

05

Operability

Skills availability, supportability, lifecycle management and operational complexity.

06

Innovation

AI readiness, data accessibility, platform extensibility and time to market.

Venue Options

Available technology venues

The right venue depends on workload characteristics, not vendor preference.

Public Cloud

Best for elastic workloads, digital services, rapid innovation and global reach.

Private Cloud

Best for predictable workloads, cost stability, controlled environments and long-term governance.

Sovereign Infrastructure

Best for regulated workloads, sensitive data and jurisdictional control.

Hybrid Architecture

Best for mixed portfolios, transitional estates and complex operating models.

AI Platforms

Best for model development, inference, advanced analytics and data-intensive innovation.

Application

Example workload placement

No two organisations are identical, but workload patterns help guide decision-making.

AI Model TrainingOften suited to specialised AI infrastructure or controlled cloud AI environments.
ERP SystemsOften suited to hybrid or controlled infrastructure depending on integration and data requirements.
Regulated DataOften suited to sovereign or tightly governed environments.
Customer PortalsOften suited to public cloud where elasticity and global access matter.
Legacy ApplicationsOften suited to private or hybrid environments when refactoring is not justified.
